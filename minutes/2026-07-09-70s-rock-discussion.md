## Summary
The group discussed the most influential vocalists of the 1970s, spanning hard rock, progressive, folk-rock, and singer-songwriter genres. Key discussions included the power and evolution of Robert Plant, the operatic range of Freddie Mercury, the ferocity of Roger Daltrey, and the technical skill of Ian Gillan. The group also highlighted significant female vocalists like Ann Wilson, Grace Slick, Stevie Nicks, and Janis Joplin, as well as "vocalist's vocalists" like Peter Hammill and the softer, more natural phrasing of singer-songwriters like James Taylor and Don Henley.
