# 70s Rock & Roll Discussion Group
**Date:** July 19, 2026
**Duration:** 8 minutes
**Attendees:** Marcus, Dana, Rick, Sheila

---

**Marcus:** Desert island albums. Five 70s rock records each, the ones you'd take if you could never listen to anything else. Dana, you've been thinking about this all night. Go.

**Dana:** I have, and it was agonizing. Okay. One — *Close to the Edge* by Yes. It's three tracks, it's ambitious, it's beautiful. I could listen to it forever and keep hearing new things. Two — *Dark Side of the Moon*. It's the complete package — sonically, emotionally, conceptually. Three — *Red* by King Crimson. It's heavy, it's complex, it's devastating. Four — *London Calling* by the Clash. For the range — rock, punk, reggae, pop. Five — Bowie's *Low*. Because I need something weird and electronic to balance the guitars.

**Rick:** Solid list. Here's mine. One — *Led Zeppelin IV*. Non-negotiable. It's the album that made me love music. Two — *Who's Next*. Because Pete Townshend is a genius and "Baba O'Riley" is the greatest rock song ever written. Three — *Paranoid* by Black Sabbath. I need heavy music on this island. Four — *At Fillmore East* by the Allman Brothers. Because I need that improvisational fire. Five — *Rumours*. Because sometimes you need perfect pop-rock songwriting, and nothing's more perfect than *Rumours*.

**Sheila:** No punk, Rick? I'm disappointed.

**Rick:** *Who's Next* is aggressive enough to scratch that itch. What's your list?

**Sheila:** One — *London Calling*. Dana and I agree on that. It's got everything. Two — *Rumours*. Rick and I agree on that. Three — *Unknown Pleasures* by Joy Division. Because Ian Curtis's voice on a desert island would keep me company in the darkest way. Four — *Horses* by Patti Smith. "Jesus died for somebody's sins but not mine" — that opening line is rock and roll. Five — *Blue* by Joni Mitchell. Because I'd need something quiet and honest, and Joni is the most honest songwriter who ever lived.

**Marcus:** Beautiful list. Here's mine. One — *Dark Side of the Moon*. Dana and I overlap there. I can't imagine a life without it. Two — *Exile on Main St.* The Stones at their rawest and most soulful. Three — *Physical Graffiti* by Led Zeppelin. It's a double album, so I'm getting more music, and it covers every style Zeppelin ever attempted. Four — *London Calling*. Three of us picked it, and rightfully so. Five — *Marquee Moon* by Television. Because Tom Verlaine's guitar would keep me from going insane on a desert island. That title track is ten minutes of pure transcendence.

**Rick:** Three out of four picked *London Calling*. I feel like I should have picked it.

**Sheila:** You should have. But your list is good too.

**Dana:** What I notice is that between the four of us, we've named sixteen different albums, and we could easily name sixteen more that deserve to be there. That's the depth of the 70s. Try doing this exercise with any other decade and it's harder.

**Marcus:** The 60s could give it a run. But I take your point. The 70s is absurdly rich.

**Rick:** What's heartbreaking about this exercise is what you leave off. I didn't pick *Dark Side of the Moon*. That feels wrong. No Bowie on my list. No Stones. You could make five completely different lists and each one would be defensible.

**Sheila:** That's the sign of a great era. It's not about consensus — it's about abundance. There's enough extraordinary music that four passionate people can't even agree on which five albums are essential.

**Dana:** And we barely touched Krautrock, or the Canterbury scene, or jazz fusion. We didn't mention Stevie Wonder, who made some of the greatest albums of the 70s. We didn't talk about Funkadelic or Parliament. The decade is bottomless.

**Marcus:** Which means we have more to talk about. Tomorrow is our last day in this series — let's do a wrap-up. Final thoughts on what the 70s mean to each of us personally.

**Sheila:** I'm going to get emotional. Fair warning.

**Rick:** Twenty days of talking about 70s rock. This has been the best thing I've done all month.

**Dana:** Agreed. See everyone for the finale tomorrow.
