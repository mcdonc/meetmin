# 70s Rock & Roll Discussion Group: Drugs and Decadence

**Date:** July 18, 2026
**Duration:** 9 minutes
**Attendees:** Marcus, Dana, Rick, Sheila
**Source:** [../meeting_dialog/2026-07-18-70s-rock-discussion.md](../meeting_dialog/2026-07-18-70s-rock-discussion.md)

## Summary
The group discussed the complex and often tragic relationship between drug use and the 70s rock era. The conversation covered the shift from psychedelic experimentation (LSD) in the early 70s to the cocaine-fueled excess of the mid-to-late 70s, as well as the devastating impact of heroin and alcoholism on artists. The group debated whether the "tortured artist" mythology justifies the personal costs and discussed how the music industry's lack of care enabled these cycles of self-destruction. They also contrasted drug-heavy eras with the disciplined professionalism of bands like Steely Dan and Rush.
