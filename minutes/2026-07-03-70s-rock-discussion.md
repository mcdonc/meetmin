## Summary
The group discussed the significance of 1971 as a landmark year for rock music, highlighting the incredible range of styles released, from the heavy sound of Black Sabbath to the intimate singer-songwriter works of Joni Mitchell and Carole King. The discussion also touched upon the tragic loss of several key figures in rock during the early 1970s, including Jim Morrison and Duane Allman, and the prolific creative output of artists like David Bowie.

## Decisions
- The group agreed that 1971 represents a peak year for rock due to its diversity and the simultaneous exploration of various musical directions.
