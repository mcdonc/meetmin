## Summary
The group discussed the merits and criticisms of progressive rock (prog rock) in the 1970s. Dana defended the genre as an expansion of rock's vocabulary, while Sheila critiqued it for prioritizing technical virtuosity over the genre's original simplicity. The discussion covered key bands like Genesis, Pink Floyd, King Crimson, and Emerson, Lake & Palmer, as well as the genre's relationship with the punk movement and the eventual emergence of post-punk.
