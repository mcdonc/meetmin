## Summary
The group discussed the defining characteristics of 1970s rock compared to the 1960s. Key themes included the evolution of production quality (multitrack recording), a shift in attitude from 60s optimism to a darker, heavier sound, and the fracturing of rock into various subgenres (prog, glam, punk, etc.). The group also noted the rise of the concept album and the evolution of instrumental proficiency in guitar, drums, and bass.

## Decisions
- The group agreed that the 1970s represented a "big tent" era where rock splintered into many distinct subgenres.
- The group acknowledged the tension between ambitious concept albums and the eventual punk backlash.
