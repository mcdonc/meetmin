## Summary
The group discussed the complex and often exploitative business landscape of 1970s rock music. Key themes included the power of managers (Peter Grant, Allen Klein), the importance of artist-owned labels (Swan Song), the ownership of master recordings, and the shift from intimate club venues to the arena rock spectacle. The discussion also touched on the impact of drug culture on artists and labels, the role of FM radio and payola, and how the industry transitioned from creative autonomy to corporate formatting.
